About

I am a planetary scientist passionate about unraveling the history of the solar system through spectroscopy, remote sensing, and data-driven analysis. My research focuses on linking laboratory, field, and orbital observations to better understand planetary differentiation, magmatic processes, and early solar system evolution. I am also interested in characterization of rocky exoplanets.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to teaching, mentorship, and advancing planetary exploration through collaborative work.

Selected Research Highlights

Lunar Meteorites as a Window into Lunar Evolution

This ongoing study focuses on characterizing the lunar meteorite NWA 11444 to investigate the formation mechanisms of magnesian anorthosites and their influence on the evolution of the lunar magma ocean.

Characterization of Artemis III Landing Sites on the Lunar South Pole

This study characterizes two sites near Shackleton, Slater, and de Gerlache craters in the lunar south pole. It maps slopes, traversable regions, potential hazards such as boulder fields, and resources including possible water ice and sunlight availability.

Effects of Space Weathering on Thermal Infrared Spectra

This study examines how space weathering influences the thermal infrared characteristics of lunar materials, using lunar swirls and young craters as natural laboratories. A key finding is that the dark lanes within lunar swirls are not ultramature but rather intermediately weathered, suggesting that magnetic field lines penetrate the lunar surface in a curved trajectory rather than perpendicularly.

Figure 3: a) Kaguya albedo map with overlayed outlines of the sampled sites. On-swirl region is outlined in pink, the dark lanes are outlined in yellow, and off-swirl region is outlined in blue. b) Scatterplot of albedo vs Christiansen Feature(CF) of the three regions c) CF image of the Reiner Gamma region overlaid on the Kaguya albedo map d)Histogram displaying the CF distributions in the on-swirl, dark lane and off-swirl regions. Relative frequency is the percentage of pixels with those CF values.

Silicic Volcanism on the Moon

This study investigates the Gruithuisen region of the Moon from both compositional and thermophysical perspectives identifying additional silicic sites in the region. It also evaluates the sensitivity of Diviner rock abundance and H-parameter maps, revealing that boulders smaller than ~2 m are not detectable in rock abundance maps but manifest as low H-parameter (high thermal inertia) regions.
