About Me

Hey, there! My name is Nandita Kumari and I am currently a PhD candidate at Vibrational Spectroscopy Lab, Stony Brook University. I am trying to constrain the bulk composition of the lunar surface via orbital images,laboratory datasets and modelling. I currently use spectroscopy (Visible-Near-Infrared and Thermal Infrared) datasets in my model. Having been on Diviner Lunar Radiometer Experiment team during grad school, my efforts are currently geared mostly towards TIR spectroscopy. In addition, I have also worked on surface characterization of three landing potential sites in the lunar south pole aiding surface exploration via rovers and crewed missions.
